Computer programming is women’s work.
Computers and the workers who operate them are believed to become vital to the state.
Computers become a symbol of industrial and social modernity. Employers expect young men to become experts in computer technology to save the economy.
Women’s representation in computing drops to 9%.
Governments attempt to push girls and women into computing, expecting it to fix the gender imbalances. The initiative to get girls, women, and people of color to train for STEM jobs cannot undo the underlying structures of power.
